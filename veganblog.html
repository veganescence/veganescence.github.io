Zero Waste Week

We’ve just realised it’s Zero Waste Week so couldn’t let the event go by without reflecting on what we do as a business and what we do in our daily lives.

Zero Waste Week happens every year in the first week in September and it’s an initiative to make us all more aware of what we throw away, why we throw our stuff out, and what happens to the waste we do throw away.

Take clothing and homewares for instance. According to WRAP UK, an astonishing £140 million worth of clothing ends up in landfill every year. The amount of carbon, chemicals, water, as well as human labour that goes into producing our clothing makes the waste even harder to bear, when much of it can be given away to the homeless, or even your family and friends (who doesn’t love a quality vintage item?) or to charity shops. Clothes that are too worn can be donated to local recycling centres to be made into new fabric.

We’ve come up with a few more suggestions about reusing precious clothing materials or old towels and duvets and things we have actually done ourselves (because we love sharing ideas and these have worked for us).
  • Old jumpers make cushions. Just add buttons, a design or even sequins and you have a reuseable item, or a wonderful handmade gift.
  • Get creative with that old pair of velvet trousers and make Christmas toys for children (just make sure there’s nothing sharp in there) 
  • Cut up old duvets and make little bags for virtually anything. You can sew by hand or on a machine and you could make a pretty cotton bag for storing underwear, or we use ours for the seeds and dried flowers and plants we forage. 
  • Old towels and tea towels make great reusable cleaning cloths. Cut them into various sizes for different jobs, and just pop them in the washing machine when they’re dirty with some soap nuts and feel very virtuous about doing your bit for the planet. Our cloths clean up any soy wax that gets split on our work surfaces.

As a business there are a range of things we like to do to reduce waste and stay cruelty-free, whilst still enjoy the finer things in life like candles and beautifully packaged goods. For instance we chose soy wax for three main reasons:

  • We source our wax from a supplier with strong sustainability credentials
  • The wax gives a clean and even burn, so there’s nothing left at the bottom of the container
  • It has a lovely mild creamy scent (ok, that’s just for the pleasure of burning candles)

Our containers:

  • We use reuseable containers (or we’ll re-fill them for you if you visit us at one of our fairs)
  • You can use our pretty tins to store paperclips, seeds, buttons, memory sticks, coins… you get the idea
  • We encourage our customers to bring in containers they no longer use and if they’re suitable we’ll refill them, sell them on and give you a discount off your next purchase (we’re just working out the mechanics of this)

Packaging and our ingredients:

  • Minimal packaging when you buy from us, the containers are still pretty
  • Cruelty-free ingredients – you'd be surprised 
  • Our wooden wicks come from sustainable forests

So enjoy getting creative during Zero Waste Week and we’d love to hear from you about the things you’re doing to make life more sustainable for humans, animals and the planet.
