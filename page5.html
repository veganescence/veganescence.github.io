Getting on your wick

We love burning our soy candles and want everyone to enjoy our beautiful candles as much as we do, in safety and comfort.

Today, we take you through the do's and dont's of candle-handling, and tell you how to get the best from your candle while staying safe and sound.

Boring? We'll try not to be. But just like the safety instructions on a plane, please pay attention - you can exit at the end of this blog...

Veganescence candles have an exceptionally clean burn from the 100% vegan soy wax we use, made from 100% soy bean  It's also produced cruelty-free, with no animal-testing.  We melt the wax then add various essential oils or synthetic fragrances (again cruelty-free, and 100% vegan), stirring gently for a consistent mix. The candles are left for the fragrance to bind to the wax, in a process called curing, which can take up to two weeks.  

At the end of the curing process, the candle scent can really be experienced over a large area when the candle is lit, which is called the 'hot throw' of a candle.  (And guess what the distance unlit candles can be detected is called? Yup, the cold throw).  Soy is of course organic, so over a period of time we've heard that it may start to degrade, although we've not seen this happening ourselves.  The scent will naturally degrade also, although one of the reasons we sell our candles in a tin, is that keeping the lid on slows this process.

Don't keep them in your cupboard too long though - we'd suggest using your candles within a year of purchase, and like any fresh product, it's best enjoyed sooner than later.

A quick note on fragrance - plant-based essential oils will degrade over time, and did you know that some become toxic when burned? Whether burning clean-burning soy wax, synthetic fragrances or essential oils combinations, you should never leave a candle unattended.

Ours have the lovely clean-burn from soy wax and are best enjoyed fresh and responsibly, so don’t keep them in the cupboard for too long and treat them with the respect all candles should be treated.There’s more to a candle that just lighting and enjoy. Your wick should be maintained for optimal burn quality.

If your candle is getting a little weak and the burn is not so bright, remember that the wick has to be maintained. Both wooden and cotton wicks need regular trimming so the burnt sections are removed and you can continue to enjoy it at its best.

For example after burning for a couple of hours, but no more than 4 for a large candle, extinguish (snuff out the flame, don’t blow) and allow the candle to cool. Depending on how long the wick is down the candle, you can then snip with a pair of scissors once it is cool so you have between 3-5mm of wick left. Remove any stray bits of wood or cotton wick.

It is now ready to light again. Enjoy the atmosphere

We’ve come up with a few more suggestions about reusing precious clothing materials or old towels and duvets and things we have actually done ourselves (because we love sharing ideas and these have worked for us).
  • Never leave a burning candle unattended and keep away from children and pets.
  • Always place the candle on a heat proof container and on a level surface before lighting.
  • Keep burning candles away from curtains and furnishings, and flammable items.
  • Allow to cool before moving to another place, as the candle may become hot. 
  • Never put a candle on warm surfaces such as electrical surface such as TV sets.
  • Burn away from draughts and direct heat sources such as open fires or electric fires. 
  • Keep wick trimmed to 0.5mm. 
  • Do not burn small candles for more than 2 hours and larger candles between 3-4 hours, then allow to cool and trim wick. Care must be taken to avoid getting debris in the wax pool. 
  • Always snuff a candle out and not blow on it to extinguish so as not to blow the molten wax. 
  • Do not burn a candle completely down, but leave a quarter of the wax in the container so the container does not overheat.

As a business there are a range of things we like to do to reduce waste and stay cruelty-free, whilst still enjoy the finer things in life like candles and beautifully packaged goods. For instance we chose soy wax for three main reasons:

  • We source our wax from a supplier with strong sustainability credentials
  • The wax gives a clean and even burn, so there’s nothing left at the bottom of the container
  • It has a lovely mild creamy scent (ok, that’s just for the pleasure of burning candles)

Our containers:

  • We use reuseable containers (or we’ll re-fill them for you if you visit us at one of our fairs)
  • You can use our pretty tins to store paperclips, seeds, buttons, memory sticks, coins… you get the idea
  • We encourage our customers to bring in containers they no longer use and if they’re suitable we’ll refill them, sell them on and give you a discount off your next purchase (we’re just working out the mechanics of this)

Packaging and our ingredients:

  • Minimal packaging when you buy from us, the containers are still pretty
  • Cruelty-free ingredients – you'd be surprised 
  • Our wooden wicks come from sustainable forests

So enjoy getting creative during Zero Waste Week and we’d love to hear from you about the things you’re doing to make life more sustainable for humans, animals and the planet.
