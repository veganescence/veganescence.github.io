Getting on your wick

We love burning our soy candles and want everyone to enjoy our beautiful candles as much as we do, in safety and comfort.

Today, we take you through the do's and dont's of candle-handling, and tell you how to get the best from your candle while staying safe and sound.

Boring? We'll try not to be. But just like the safety instructions on a plane, please pay attention - you can exit at the end of this blog...

Veganescence candles have an exceptionally clean burn from the 100% vegan soy wax we use, made from 100% soy bean  It's also produced cruelty-free, with no animal-testing.  We melt the wax then add various essential oils or synthetic fragrances (again cruelty-free, and 100% vegan), stirring gently for a consistent mix. The candles are left for the fragrance to bind to the wax, in a process called curing, which can take up to two weeks.  

At the end of the curing process, the candle scent can really be experienced over a large area when the candle is lit, which is called the 'hot throw' of a candle.  (And guess what the distance unlit candles can be detected is called? Yup, the cold throw).  Soy is of course organic, so over a period of time we've heard that it may start to degrade, although we've not seen this happening ourselves.  The scent will naturally degrade also, although one of the reasons we sell our candles in a tin, is that keeping the lid on slows this process.

Don't keep them in your cupboard too long though - we'd suggest using your candles within a year of purchase, and like any fresh product, it's best enjoyed sooner than later.

A quick note on fragrance - plant-based essential oils will degrade over time, and did you know that some become toxic when burned? Because typically a candle will only take up around 10% of fragrance, in the past we've used synthetic fragrances to get the throw we require, and help stop the candle scent from fading.  We've stopped doing this now - we're taking time out to work with florals, and botanicals to develop a new range.     

Now, sorry to break this to you, but the relaxing scented candle you bought needs some maintenance too, as improper use can reduce the burn time. Leaving it in a draft, or near heat, not keeping the wick trimmed, or burning it for too long, can all contribute to a shortened candle life.

The first time you burn our candles, they should be allowed to achieve a full melt pool across the top of the candle and from then on, the melted wax will be consistent and shouldn't tunnel.  Burning the candle too frequently for a shorter period is the main cause of tunnelling.

If your candle is getting a little weak and the burn is not so bright, remember that the wick has to be maintained. Both wooden and cotton wicks need regular trimming to remove the burnt sections are removed so you can continue to enjoy it at its best.  Cotton candle wicks should be trimmed to about 3-5 mm, wooden wicks trimmed to about 10 mm (nail-clippers are great for this!)

Competition time! Take a look at the following list of do's and don'ts - it's just common-sense right? Well, read through the list and play 'bad-habit bingo' and see if there's anything on the list you don't do that you should, or do do that you shouldn't! Then change. 
  • Never leave a burning candle unattended and keep well away from children and pets. Seriously. Cats, dogs, children - they're all curious creatures.
  • Always place the candle on a heat proof container and a level surface. The candle container has a flame in it - it's going to get hot...
  • Keep burning candles away from curtains and furnishings, and flammable items. A candle flame can move in a breeze, as can curtains or window nets - be safe, keep a large exclusion zone around your candle.
  • Allow to cool before moving to another place, as the candle container may become hot. 
  • Allow soy candle to cool and harden before relighting. 
  • Never put a candle on warm surfaces such as electrical surface e.g. on top of TV sets.
  • Burn away from draughts and direct heat sources such as open fires or electric fires. 
  • Keep cotton wick trimmed to 0.5 cm, wooden wicks trimmed to 1cm. 
  • Do not burn small candles for more than 2 hours and larger candles for between 3-4 hours (then allow to cool, and trim the wick).
  • Care must be taken to avoid getting debris in the wax pool - you may want to pull any flowers away from the wick, especially towards the end of the candles life. 
  • Always snuff a candle out and don't blow on it to extinguish so you don't blow the molten wax. 
  • Don't burn a candle completely down, but leave a small amount of the wax in the container so the container does not overheat. 
  • Harmful if swallowed - if anyone ingests this, call a doctor, and rinse the mouth out.
  • Wash hands thoroughly after handling.
  • Do not eat, drink or smoke when using this product.
  • Avoid release to the environment.
  • Retain all safety information for future use. 

So enjoy your lovely scented candles, but especially as the holiday season approaches, please, please, please be responsible, stay careful, and have a great Christmas.
